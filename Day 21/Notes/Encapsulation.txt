What is Encapsulation?

Encapsulation is the OOP principle of:

Bundling data (variables) and methods (functions) together inside a class and restricting direct access to some of the object’s components.

In simple words:

Hide internal details

Expose only what is necessary

Protect data from accidental modification

🎯 2. Why Do We Need Encapsulation?

Without encapsulation:

Anyone can modify variables directly

No control over validation

Hard to maintain code

Data integrity can break

With encapsulation:

Data is protected

Validation can be enforced

Code becomes secure and maintainable


Real-Life Example
🏦 Bank Account System

You cannot directly change your bank balance.

You must:

Deposit money

Withdraw money

The bank hides the balance variable and allows only controlled access.

That is encapsulation.
